5.What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive eperience?

With the website being named 7 poems so I guess it was quite straightforward that this is the compilation of different poems that the creator of the website wants to share ( or that person might be the author as well ). But with a such simple concept like that, the presentation here felt a bit abstract and raised a lot of curiosity for the audiences with the music incorporated into the text keep changing. I even theorize that with the right combination, you might get a harmonized music soundtrack or a complete message of the website.

6.What is your impression of how the interactive experience communicates its primary goal?

I think the website communicates its goal quite well if the goal is like what I'm speculated which is being confused and abstract. While the website itself might seem simple and basic at face value, when you consider the content that it tries to convey, and if you want to understand the context here, I think you might have to dig quite far into the rabbit hole of this interactive interface.

7.What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

I think this experience is meant to be diggest through multiple interactions through different tries. At first, you might get frustrated and might be disturbed about what's happening with all of the color and 80s vibe beats and all of the nonsense text. But there's some immersive element to the website that keeps you intrigued and want to explore more on what exactly all of this is about 7 different poems, is there a connection between them or it's all just nonsense? So in my experience, you might have to spend quite a long time on this website for like a couple of hours or so, and through plenty of re-try to keep track and write down all of the text and the combination that the boxes created. It should be like solving a puzzle, an enigma, or some sort.

8.What is your impression of how the interactive experience communicates how it should be interacted with over time?

As mentioned above the experience communicates in a way that makes the user somewhat disturbed at first but starts to get intrigued by the experience of finding out the mystery or the context of the website. But this also depends on the user whether they want to keep digging or if this is just too much of a time investment for them to care too much for, then they might quit. It's a time-consuming process to understand the web page and took a lot more work rather than just scrolling through the web, you have to note it all down and try to make sense of it, a thought-provoking process that might not suitable for everyone.
